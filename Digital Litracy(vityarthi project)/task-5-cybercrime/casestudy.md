## **IDENTITY THEFT AND CYBER SCAMS**



##### Identity theft and cyber scams don’t just happen to careless people, they can happen to anyone and that is why it is becoming a more and more serious issue. The truth is, scammers are really good at one thing: earning your trust and empathy quickly. They might pretend to be your bank, a delivery company, or even a government official. And before you even realize what’s happening, you’ve shared something important like your password, an OTP, or your Aadhaar number.

##### It usually doesn’t feel like a scam at first. It starts small. Maybe it’s a normal-looking message, a harmless link, or a polite phone call from someone who sounds genuine and helpful. But while it feels casual to us, they’re quietly collecting bits of our information to use against us. And once they have enough, things can go wrong very quickly. Money can disappear from your account, orders can be placed in your name, or in the worst case scenario your identity might be used for loans or illegal activities.

##### Students, senior citizens, and people new to the internet are often targeted, but honestly, anyone can fall for it. Even experienced users can make a mistake on a busy day or during a moment of distraction, because they are just that well trained. Practicing this over long periods of time makes them familiar with ways that are easy to fool people.

##### A few months ago, there was a case where an aged lady was stuck in a scam for two days straight. The scammers had done their research and pretended to call from the passport agency asking for money and bank details, as she was constantly inquiring about the same. She lost around 20-30 lakhs in that scam. 

##### When this happens, it’s not just about losing money. There’s confusion, stress, and even a sense of guilt. It can take time to feel safe online again.

##### The good news? Staying safe doesn’t require complicated steps. Just pause and think before sharing sensitive information, use strong passwords, and always remember, no genuine bank or service will ever ask for your OTP.

##### Moreover focus on what sites you are visiting, what links you are opening. Try and keep your personal information private as much as possible.
